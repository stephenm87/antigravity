Today's Debate Question

Is external intervention justified under the Responsibility to Protect, or does it violate state sovereignty?

What Is R2P?

The Responsibility to Protect (R2P) is an international norm adopted by the UN in 2005. It says that every state has the primary responsibility to protect its own population from mass atrocities: genocide, war crimes, ethnic cleansing, and crimes against humanity. When a state fails to do this, the international community has a responsibility to step in.

Pillar 1

The state must protect its own people from mass atrocities.

Pillar 2

The international community should help states fulfill Pillar 1.

Pillar 3

If a state fails, the international community must intervene — diplomatically first, then by force if necessary.

The Two Cases Being Debated

Venezuela

A deep humanitarian crisis under the Maduro government, with allegations of crimes against humanity. The R2P question: is external intervention (legal or extrajudicial) justified to protect the population, or does it violate Venezuela's sovereign independence?

Iran

Israeli/US strikes against Iranian military and industrial targets, framed as preemptive or retaliatory. The R2P question: can "protection of regional security" or "prevention of future crimes" be mapped onto R2P principles, or is this classic state-on-state power politics?

What to Do at Each Phase
Openings
Write down: "What is the core disagreement?" in one sentence.
Pause 1 (2 min)
Ask 1 clarifying question each. Use the stems below.
Arguments
Note the strongest evidence from each side. Write down anything you don't understand.
Pause 2 (3 min)
Ask 1–2 questions probing the evidence. Push for specifics.
Cross-Exam
Note who handles pressure better. Who answers directly? Who deflects?
Rebuttals
Track which side's rebuttal is more effective. Start forming your opinion.
Closings
Listen carefully — this is the last input before your verdict.
Pause 3 (3 min)
Deliver your verdict. State which side wins and cite 1–2 specific moments.
Question Stems for Pauses
Clarifying

"Can you explain what you mean by...?"

"When you say R2P / sovereignty, what does that look like?"

Probing Evidence

"How do you know that?"

"What would the other side say about that?"

Connections

"How does this connect to what the other team said?"

"Does this apply beyond this one case?"

Challenge

"What's the strongest argument against your position?"

"What would happen if the opposite were true?"
