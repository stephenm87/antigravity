Rating Quick Guide
What does each score look like? Use this as a reference while you evaluate.
Reference Sheet
This guide helps you calibrate your ratings. For each category, read across to see what a 1, 2, 3, or 4 looks like in practice.
Category 1 Getting There 2 Good 3 Great 4 Outstanding
🌍 Community Impact The project addresses a need, but the impact on others is unclear or limited. The project benefits others and shows some understanding of the community's needs. The project creates a meaningful, measurable difference for a specific community. The project drives lasting change, with clear evidence of deep community understanding and engagement.
🤝 Teamwork Participation is uneven; some members seem to carry the work. Team members contribute, but roles and responsibilities aren't always clear. Everyone contributes consistently and supports each other's ideas respectfully. The team elevates each other — building on ideas, resolving disagreements, and operating like a well-oiled machine.
🎤 Storytelling Facts are shared, but the presentation feels flat or reads from notes. Ideas are communicated clearly enough, with some engaging moments. The presentation tells a compelling story — visuals and speech work together with confidence. The audience is captivated — the presentation feels professional, polished, and emotionally resonant.
🧠 Deep Thinking Basic understanding is shown, but analysis stays on the surface. Ideas are explained with some connections, though reasoning could go deeper. Evidence from multiple sources is used to draw logical, well-supported conclusions. Analysis reveals patterns, counterexamples, and nuance — going well beyond what was expected.
💡 Creative Approach The project follows a standard or predictable approach. There are some interesting choices, though the approach is mostly conventional. The project takes a fresh, effective approach that clearly fits its goals. The project is highly original — showing creative risk-taking that leads to a uniquely compelling result.
Tip: A score of 3 ("Great") represents strong, grade-level performance. Reserve 4 ("Outstanding") for truly exceptional work.
