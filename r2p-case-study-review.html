Reading Recall · ~8 minCase Study Knowledge Check

10 questions drawn from your Venezuela and Iran readings. No notes — test what stuck.

Question 1 · Venezuela
What was the official US name for the operation that extracted Maduro from Caracas?
Operation Absolute Resolve was the name given to the US military extraction of Maduro and his wife from Caracas to face narco-terrorism charges in New York.
Question 2 · Venezuela
What criminal charges did the US bring against Maduro?
The US used its own federal indictments charging Maduro with narco-terrorism — notably NOT humanitarian charges under international law, which is why critics question the R2P applicability.
Question 3 · Venezuela
Which Venezuelan security force was linked to extrajudicial killings during the humanitarian crisis?
The FAES (Fuerzas de Acciones Especiales) were repeatedly cited by the UN and human rights organisations for extrajudicial killings — a key piece of evidence for arguing crimes against humanity existed.
Question 4 · Venezuela
Which UN Charter article do critics cite when arguing the Venezuela extraction violated international law?
Article 2(4) is the foundational prohibition on using force against another state's territorial integrity. The unilateral extraction, without UNSC authorisation, is argued to violate this article directly.
Question 5 · Venezuela
A Constructivist analysing the Venezuela operation would most likely focus on:
Constructivists examine how language, identity, and framing shape political reality. The "narco-terrorist" label is a Constructivist framing device — it redefines a sovereign leader as a criminal, making intervention seem justified.
Question 6 · Iran
What were the names of the joint US-Israel operations that struck Iran in February 2026?
The US code-named its strikes Operation Epic Fury; Israel's component was Operation Roaring Lion. Together they constituted one of the most dramatic decapitation strikes in modern history.
Question 7 · Iran
What was the most significant leadership target eliminated in the Iran strikes?
The strikes eliminated Supreme Leader Khamenei — the highest political and religious authority in Iran. This is why critics describe it as an assassination of a sovereign head of state, shattering international norms.
Question 8 · Iran
Why do critics argue the Iran strikes CANNOT be classified as R2P?
R2P's four trigger situations are genocide, war crimes, ethnic cleansing, and crimes against humanity. Nuclear proliferation, however dangerous, falls outside this framework — making R2P inapplicable to the Iran strikes.
Question 9 · Iran
What "liberation" rhetoric did the US use to partially frame the Iran strikes?
The "liberation" language borrows from R2P rhetoric — framing the strikes as freeing an oppressed population. Constructivists see this as evidence that R2P's normative language persists even when its institutional requirements are ignored.
Question 10 · Iran
What was the immediate consequence of the Iran strikes for regional human security?
The strikes triggered retaliatory barrages against US military bases and Gulf states, causing mass casualties and regional destabilisation — the very opposite of advancing human security, which is the core paradox for Paper 2.

🟢 Explanation

High-Band Exemplar · Venezuela PEEL Paragraph

The capture of Nicolás Maduro in January 2026 demonstrates that R2P has been rendered functionally obsolete by the strategic priorities of powerful states. Operation Absolute Resolve was a unilateral US military extraction that bypassed the UN Security Council entirely, despite Venezuela's humanitarian crisis — including mass displacement and extrajudicial killings by the FAES — arguably meeting the threshold for R2P Pillar III activation. From a Realist perspective, the operation was driven by US national security interests — specifically counter-narcotics enforcement and hemispheric hegemony — rather than by any genuine commitment to the humanitarian norm. The fact that the US framed the intervention as a law-enforcement action against a "narco-terrorist" rather than as a humanitarian mission reveals, as a Constructivist would argue, that R2P's language was deliberately avoided because invoking it would have required multilateral legitimacy the US did not seek. Therefore, this case strongly supports the claim that R2P is obsolete: even where a genuine humanitarian crisis exists, powerful states choose unilateral action driven by strategic interest, rendering the norm's institutional requirements irrelevant in practice.

✅ Examiner note: This paragraph names the theory (Realism, Constructivism), uses specific evidence (FAES, UNSC bypass, narco-terrorist framing), distinguishes state security from human security, and links back to the prompt with a clear evaluative position. This is what a 13–15 mark body paragraph looks like.
