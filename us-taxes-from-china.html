Expat Tax Guide

Filing U.S. Taxes from China Without Your PIN

Electronic solutions when you can't reach the IRS by phone and mailing paper returns isn't an option.

1
Fastest Self-File Fix Use Prior-Year AGI Instead of PIN

The IRS allows you to authenticate your e-file using your prior-year Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) instead of remembering your old Self-Select PIN.

Steps

  1. Open your tax software (TurboTax, TaxAct, Free File, etc.)
  2. When prompted for your PIN, select "I don't remember my PIN" or "Use AGI instead"
  3. Enter your prior-year AGI from Line 11 of your last Form 1040
  4. If you don't have your return:
    • Try IRS Get Transcript Online (may require identity verification)
    • Or ask your preparer if they have a copy from last year
ⓘ

First-time filers or no prior return? Enter $0 for AGI — the IRS system accepts this.

2
Best If Working With a Preparer Use the Practitioner PIN Method

If you're using a tax professional, they can bypass your forgotten PIN entirely.

How It Works

  • Your preparer uses the Practitioner PIN method, which does not require your prior-year AGI or old PIN
  • You sign Form 8879 (IRS e-file Signature Authorization) to authorize them to enter a PIN on your behalf
  • The 5-digit PIN is chosen at that moment — you don't need to recall an old one

Remote Signing from China

  • Your preparer can email or fax Form 8879 to you
  • You can print, hand-sign, scan, and return it via email — or use Adobe Acrobat / other e-sign tools (IRS permits electronic signatures for Form 8879)
  • No international mailing required
➤

Tip: Use WeChat or WhatsApp to coordinate with your preparer for faster document exchange.

3
Available in English Use IRS Live Chat Instead of Phone

If you need IRS help but phone lines are unreachable from China:

4
Buy Yourself More Time File an Extension Electronically

If you're stuck and the deadline is near:

  1. File Form 4868 (extension) electronically using AGI or a new PIN
  2. This extends your filing deadline to October 15 (automatic for overseas taxpayers; payment still due April 15)
  3. Use the extra time to retrieve transcripts or resolve PIN issues
◆

Note: U.S. citizens and residents abroad automatically receive a 2-month extension to June 16 to file and pay, but interest accrues on unpaid balances after April 15.

Last Resort If All Else Fails: Ship It

If electronic options truly fail and you must paper-file:

  • Use DHL, FedEx, or UPS (not China Post) — these are recognized as "timely mailing" by the IRS
  • Include a note: "Filed from abroad — e-file authentication unavailable"
  • Keep tracking info and proof of mailing date

Bottom Line

You likely don't need your old PIN at all. Use your prior-year AGI to e-file now, or have your preparer use the Practitioner PIN method with a remotely signed Form 8879. Both are fully electronic and work from anywhere in the world.
