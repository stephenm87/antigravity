Biographical Profiles
A
  • Became the head of state of China (Chairman of the PRC) from 1959–1968
  • Was officially called Mao's "closest comrade in arms"
  • Instituted economic reforms that were more moderate than Mao's radical ideas
  • Became a victim of the Cultural Revolution — denounced as a "capitalist roader," put on house arrest, and died in 1969
B
  • A younger member of the party who joined after the Long March
  • Was a supporter of more moderate ideas and the reform ideas of Liu Shaoqi and Deng Xiaoping
  • Was denounced by Red Guards during the Cultural Revolution and paraded around in a dunce cap but survived
  • Was the premier of China from 1980–1987 and would bring in capitalist reforms
C
  • Founder of the People's Republic of China
  • Ruled as the Chairman of the Chinese Communist Party from its establishment in 1949 until his death in 1976
  • Was celebrated in a cult of personality
  • Put forward the Great Leap Forward and the Cultural Revolution
D
  • The Premier of China from 1949 until his death in 1976
  • Was known for helping to craft China's foreign policy
  • Managed to avoid being purged and balanced staying on Mao's good side with implementing policy he believed in
  • Is considered to have been a stabilizing force during the Cultural Revolution, trying to curb the efforts of radicals
E
  • Went to military academy and was responsible for disseminating Maoist ideology within the People's Liberation Army
  • One of the most influential people in the Cultural Revolution — Mao's chosen successor
  • Died in a mysterious plane crash in 1971 over Mongolia, allegedly while attempting to flee after a failed coup
  • Played a key role in promoting the "Little Red Book" and the cult of Mao
