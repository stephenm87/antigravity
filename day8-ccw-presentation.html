Founding of the People's Republic of China

Focus Question

What factors contributed to the success of the CCP and the downfall of the GMD?

Mao Zedong proclaiming the PRC, Tiananmen, Oct. 1 1949 — Wikimedia Commons (Public Domain)

  • October 1st 1949: Founding of People's Republic of China (PRC)
    • "Liberation" (解放) – Mao Zedong gave a triumphant victory speech in Beijing while crowds cheered and watched a procession of the PLA and other party members.
  • Chiang fled with remaining GMD army to Taiwan to found the Republic of China (ROC)
    • Claimed to be the legitimate Chinese government
    • Until 1971, Taiwan was recognized as the only legitimate government of China by the United Nations

The impact of WWII on the Chinese Civil War

  • CCP claimed they more effectively defended China from Japan, while the KMT efforts were described as "half-hearted"
  • KMT received financial/military support from USA and Great Britain, which gave Chiang legitimacy, but also made him look like a sell out to the West
  • Chiang expected a long land war with US troops to buy him time to solidify his position against the CCP, which didn't happen after the atomic bomb.

The Chinese Civil War Resumed

  • Between 1946-47, the Nationalists made gains in cities and the north and pushed the Communists out of Yan'an.
  • In 1946, Mao reorganized communist forces in a single army People's Liberation Army (PLA)
    • Developed successful guerrilla tactics and took control of Manchuria
  • By mid-1947, the PLA turned to conventional warfare and pushed into Nationalist strongholds in the center and west.

Chinese Civil War map — Wikimedia Commons (U.S. Government, Public Domain)

Developing a Complex Thesis

  • A thesis statement is a specific roadmap to a historical argument, not a short-answer response
    • An evaluation or overall answer to the question
    • A line of reasoning that shows the moves you will make in your essay
      • Each point in your line of reasoning should be a category of evidence, not a single piece of evidence (think broad buckets that contain pieces of evidence INSIDE)
  • Thesis statements are created by first analyzing and organizing evidence into buckets
    • Group your evidence into groups, then name the category and develop a claim about that group
    • Once you develop your specific groups, you can see if you have complexity
      • Multiple groups about a PIECES category, positive or negative categories, continuities or changes, similarities or differences, or some other division that fits your question

What factors contributed to the success of the CCP and downfall of the KMT in Mainland China?

  • Work with your table to organize the evidence into three or four groups.
  • Once you organize the evidence, paraphrase the evidence on the handout and create a claim for each group. Be sure your claim includes a name for that group.
    • The [GMD/CCP] [won/lost] because…
    • As you write your claims, think about next-level organization — are your claims political (including military), economic, social?
  • When you're finished, I will give you a specific prompt that you can respond to with a thesis statement.
