Lesson Overview Learning Objectives & Framework
🎓 Lesson Objective

You will evaluate the tension between state sovereignty, state security, and external intervention by analysing the theoretical framework of the Responsibility to Protect (R2P) against the January 2026 capture of Nicolás Maduro and the February 2026 US/Israel strikes on Iran.

🔐

HL Global Challenge: Security

This lesson centres on the critical distinction between State Security (protecting the nation from external/internal threats) and Human Security (protecting individuals from violence and human rights abuses). This tension is precisely what makes both 2026 interventions such rich analytical subjects.

🏛️

Realism

States act in self-interest; power and security drive intervention not humanitarian norms.

🌐

Liberalism

International institutions and norms (like R2P) shape state behaviour and legitimise intervention.

💬

Constructivism

Norms, identities, and language matter how states frame intervention shapes its legitimacy.

Operation Absolute Resolve Venezuela

US military extraction of President Nicolás Maduro from Caracas to New York on narco-terrorism charges.

February 28, 2026

Operation Epic Fury / Roaring Lion Iran

Massive US-Israel joint aerial bombardment across Iran; Supreme Leader Khamenei eliminated.

Theoretical Foundation The Responsibility to Protect (R2P)

Before analysing current events, you must grasp what R2P entails under international law and how it intersects with human security.

🏳️

Pillar I State Responsibility

Every state has the primary responsibility to protect its own population from genocide, war crimes, ethnic cleansing, and crimes against humanity.

🤝

Pillar II International Assistance

The international community must assist states in building their capacity to protect their populations, and support prevention efforts.

⚖️

Pillar III Timely Collective Action

When a state manifestly fails, the international community through the UN Security Council has a responsibility for timely and decisive collective action.

🤔

The Central Debate

Does R2P legitimise unilateral "regime change" when human security collapses? Or is it strictly a humanitarian mechanism requiring UN Security Council authorisation? This is the analytical question you must wrestle with throughout this lesson.

✏️ Article Annotation Guide — Read With Purpose

Use this guide for all readings and case study sources in this lesson. As you read, highlight or annotate moments where you spot any of the following. Not every category will appear in every article — that absence is itself evidence worth noting.

🔐 Security
  • Is the article framing security as state security (protecting the nation) or human security (protecting individuals)?
  • Who or what is identified as the threat? To whom?
  • Does the intervention make people safer or less safe?
🏛️ R2P Principles
  • Which pillar of R2P is referenced or violated?
    (I: State protects; II: International assistance; III: Collective action)
  • Was UN Security Council authorisation sought or bypassed?
  • Does the article argue R2P is alive, eroding, or dead?
🌐 Theories in Action
  • Realism: Does the author emphasise self-interest, power, or survival? Does the state act without international approval?
  • Liberalism: Are institutions, norms, or multilateral cooperation presented as solutions?
  • Constructivism: How does language or framing shape the legitimacy of the action?
⚖️ Core Themes
  • Power: Which states hold power here? How is it exercised?
  • Legitimacy: Does the author present the action as legal, moral, or neither?
  • Human Rights: Are rights invoked to justify or to critique the intervention?
  • Peace and Conflict: Does the action reduce or escalate conflict?
💡 Annotation tip: Use one colour per category (e.g. blue = security, green = R2P, yellow = theory, purple = theme). A short margin note — even one word — is more useful than underlining alone.
🔍 Discussion Questions — R2P Foundation
  • Under what conditions, if any, does R2P permit unilateral military action?
  • Why is Pillar III dependent on the UN Security Council? What are the structural limitations this creates?
  • Can a norm survive when the world's most powerful states ignore its procedural requirements?
  • How does the concept of human security change the traditional, state-centric view of sovereignty?

Case Study 1 · January 2026 The Capture of Nicolás Maduro
🌎

Operation Absolute Resolve

In early January 2026, the US launched a unilateral military extraction of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro and his wife from Caracas, transporting them to New York to face narco-terrorism charges under US federal indictments.

⬛ Perspective A

The Hegemonic / State Security Justification

The US framed this not strictly as an R2P humanitarian mission, but as a necessary security and anti-narcotics operation. A state run by a "narco-terrorist" forfeits sovereign immunity removing the leadership protects both US state security and Venezuelan human security.

State Security Realism Counter-terrorism Executive Authority
📰
Brookings Institution

Making sense of the US military operation in Venezuela

Explores the broad executive authority claimed and implications for democratic transition.
⬜ Perspective B

The Sovereignty / Anti-Interventionist Critique

Critics and international law experts argue this violates Article 2(4) of the UN Charter. From a Realist perspective, it is unilateral regime change disguised as justice bypassing international security architectures to assert hegemony in the Western Hemisphere.

UN Charter Art. 2(4) Sovereignty Hegemony International Law
🏛️
House of Commons Library

The US capture of Nicolás Maduro

Objective breakdown of the operation, casualties, international response, and international law questions.
🔍 Analytical Lens: Applying R2P Criteria to Venezuela
  • Was there a manifest failure to protect? Venezuela's humanitarian crisis (2015–2026) involved mass displacement, extrajudicial killings, and starvation potentially meeting the threshold.
  • Was UN Security Council authorisation sought? No the operation was entirely unilateral, bypassing the UNSC.
  • Was the stated aim humanitarian? The framing was counter-narcotics and state security, not explicitly humanitarian blurring R2P's applicability.
  • What does the "narco-terror" framing mean for sovereignty? Does a state lose sovereign protections when its leadership is designated a criminal enterprise?
✏️ Annotation Reminder — Venezuela Sources

As you read the Brookings and House of Commons Library articles, annotate for: security framing (state vs. human), R2P pillar violations, Realist or Constructivist reasoning, and the themes of power and legitimacy. Ask: is the "narco-terrorist" label a legal argument, a Realist move, or a Constructivist framing device?

💬 Discussion Questions — Venezuela
  • Did the Venezuelan humanitarian crisis constitute a "manifest failure" sufficient to trigger R2P Pillar III?
  • Does labelling Maduro a "narco-terrorist" legally change his status under international law?
  • How might a Realist, a Liberal institutionalist, and a Constructivist each characterise Operation Absolute Resolve?
  • What precedent does this set for other US adversaries accused of criminality?

Case Study 2 · February 28, 2026 US and Israel Strikes on Iran
💥

Operation Epic Fury / Roaring Lion

On February 28, 2026, the US and Israel launched a massive joint aerial bombardment across Iran. The strikes eliminated Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and targeted widespread military infrastructure making it one of the most dramatic decapitation strikes in modern history.

⬛ Perspective A

The Preemptive / "Liberation" Justification

The US and Israel argue decapitation strikes were a preemptive necessity to halt imminent threats and degrade Iran's nuclear programme. Rhetoric calling it a chance for the Iranian people to "take back" their country blends Realist preemptive defence with a liberal-democratic liberation narrative echoing R2P language.

Preemptive Defence Non-proliferation Realism Liberation Narrative
⚔️
Institute for the Study of War (ISW)

Iran Update Special Report: US and Israeli Strikes, February 28, 2026

Details military objectives, the decapitation strategy, and the goal of toppling the Islamic Republic.
⬜ Perspective B

The Escalation / Violation of Norms Critique

Opponents view this as an unprecedented act of war bypassing the UNSC entirely. The unilateral assassination of a sovereign head of state shatters international norms, risking a catastrophic regional war and devastating civilian security the very opposite of human security promotion.

UN Charter Violation Norm Erosion Civilian Harm Regional Destabilisation
🌍
Council on Foreign Relations (CFR)

Gauging the Impact of Massive U.S.-Israeli Strikes on Iran

Covers civilian impact, the retaliatory barrage against US bases and Gulf states, and Middle East implications.
🔍 Analytical Lens: Applying R2P Criteria to Iran
  • Was the stated aim human protection? Officially, the aim was nuclear non-proliferation and preemptive state security not Pillar III R2P activation.
  • Does R2P cover a nuclear threat? R2P as written applies to genocide, war crimes, ethnic cleansing, and crimes against humanity not nuclear proliferation.
  • Was this preemptive self-defence (Art. 51) or aggression? This is the crux of the international law debate.
  • What happened to civilian security? If strikes produce regional war and mass casualties, do they advance or undermine human security?
✏️ Annotation Reminder — Iran Sources

As you read the ISW Special Report and CFR brief, annotate for: security framing (preemptive state security vs. civilian human security), R2P pillar bypassed, Realist justification vs. Constructivist liberation framing, and the themes of legitimacy, human rights, and peace and conflict. Ask: does the "liberation" rhetoric make this a Constructivist use of R2P language, even when the institutional requirements were ignored?

💬 Discussion Questions — Iran
  • Does the "liberation" framing of the strikes borrow from R2P language? Is this appropriate or cynical?
  • Can preemptive strikes on nuclear infrastructure be justified under existing international law?
  • How does the killing of a sitting Supreme Leader compare to other targeted assassinations in recent history?
  • Who decides when retaliatory consequences become acceptable "collateral damage" and who bears the cost?

Paper 2 Application & Activity Sovereignty vs. Security Debate

Synthesise this lesson by conducting a "Sovereignty vs. Security" debate using R2P criteria. Did either 2026 intervention meet the threshold for R2P? Or do they represent a return to pure Realism where powerful states act unilaterally based on national security interests?

Paper 2 Practice Prompt

"The concept of the Responsibility to Protect (R2P) has been rendered obsolete by the strategic security interests of powerful states." Evaluate this claim using two contemporary examples.

You should draw on Venezuela (January 2026) and Iran (February 2026) as their two contemporary examples, applying Realism, Liberalism, and Constructivism where appropriate.

🔴 Claim · Realism

R2P Is Obsolete

Recent events in Venezuela and Iran show interventions are driven by state security, counter-terrorism, and regional hegemony not humanitarian norms. The UNSC was bypassed entirely in both cases, revealing that Pillar III is functionally dead.

🟢 Counter-Claim · Constructivism

Humanitarian Language Persists

Human rights and liberation narratives were still deployed to legitimise both actions. Even if the execution is unilateral, the continued use of human security rhetoric demonstrates that R2P retains its normative moral weight perhaps evolving rather than dying.

📝 Activity Outline

  1. Deconstruct the Prompt

    Have you identify and define all key concepts: R2P, strategic interests, security (state vs. human), power, sovereignty. You annotate the prompt before writing begins.

  2. Choose Your Perspective

    You decide their own argument stance before writing: do they claim R2P has been rendered obsolete by state power, or that the norm is evolving rather than dying? You record their chosen position and the theory (Realism, Liberalism, or Constructivism) that best supports it. This decision drives everything that follows.

  3. Outline Construction

    You build a three-paragraph outline that reflects their chosen stance: (A) the theoretical basis for their position, (B) Venezuela as supporting evidence, and (C) Iran as supporting evidence or a managed counter-claim. The outline must make their argument clear from the first paragraph.

  4. Extended Response (Homework)

    You write a full Paper 2 extended response (800-1,000 words) using their outline, arguing consistently from their chosen perspective. Peer-assessed against the IB Global Politics mark scheme.

🗓️

Next Week: Sovereignty vs. Security Debate

After essays are submitted, you are paired with a peer who argued the opposite position. Each defends their written stance in a structured exchange, using evidence and theory labels (e.g., "From a Constructivist perspective..."). Having already committed their argument to writing, you arrive at the debate prepared and accountable.

Assessment Tip

Strong Paper 2 responses engage explicitly with theory labels, show clear claim-counter-claim structure, and evaluate both case studies with specific evidence. Remind you: evaluation means taking a justified position, not merely describing both sides.

📋 Paragraph Comparison: Venezuela High-Scoring vs Average
✦ High-Scoring Response

Topic Sentence: The US capture of Nicolás Maduro in January 2026 illustrates that strategic state security interests rather than humanitarian principles drove the intervention, demonstrating the systemic weakening of R2P as an autonomous multilateral norm.

Evidence & Link: O'Hanlon and Piccone at the Brookings Institution document that "Operation Absolute Resolve" was justified under broad executive counter-narcotics authority, with no request for UN Security Council authorisation a procedural requirement fundamental to R2P's Pillar III. Similarly, the House of Commons Library's briefing CBP-10452 confirms the operation bypassed all established multilateral mechanisms, framing it as a law-enforcement action rather than a humanitarian intervention.

Analysis (Realism + Constructivism): From a Realist perspective, the US was asserting Western Hemisphere hegemony under the guise of legality the "narco-terrorist" designation serving as a rhetorical device to strip Maduro of sovereign immunity and circumvent UN Charter Article 2(4). Constructivists would add that this language appropriates the moral grammar of R2P without its institutional constraints, normalising unilateral action as a substitute for collective security. The operation therefore did not activate R2P it strategic­ally borrowed its legitimising rhetoric while discarding its procedural architecture.

Link to Prompt: This supports the claim that R2P has been selectively weaponised: the norm's language persists as a legitimising tool precisely because it retains rhetorical power, yet its institutional requirements are abandoned whenever they constrain powerful states.

🎯 Examiner note: Two sourced evidence points integrated into one paragraph, dual-theory analysis, and a nuanced link that goes beyond simply agreeing with the prompt.

📋 Paragraph Comparison: Iran High-Scoring vs Average
✦ High-Scoring Response

Topic Sentence: The February 2026 US-Israel strikes on Iran reveal a paradox at the heart of the prompt: while R2P's institutional framework was entirely bypassed, its normative language was deliberately mobilised to legitimise the action suggesting the norm is not obsolete but rather in a state of deep structural crisis.

Evidence & Link: The Institute for the Study of War's Special Report of 28 February 2026 details the elimination of Supreme Leader Khamenei and strikes across Iranian military infrastructure a decapitation strategy explicitly framed by US officials as a chance for "the Iranian people to take back their country." The Council on Foreign Relations' expert brief of the same date documents the retaliatory barrage against US bases and Gulf states, demonstrating that civilian security across the region was severely threatened by the very operation nominally framed in humanitarian terms.

Analysis (Constructivism + Realism): From a Constructivist standpoint, the "liberation" framing is significant: states continue to invoke R2P's moral grammar because the norm retains persuasive power. However, from a Realist perspective, UNSC authorisation was never sought because it was never the point the strikes served Israeli and US strategic interests in disabling Iran's nuclear programme and eliminating its supreme leadership. R2P's three pillars were not activated; they were mimicked.

Link to Prompt: Rather than rendering R2P obsolete, the Iran case exposes its structural fragility: the norm's language survives where it is useful to great powers, while its procedural constraints are discarded when they are not a condition better described as weaponised instrumentalisation than death.

🎯 Examiner note: Two linked sources with specific dates and content, counterintuitive topic sentence, two-theory analysis that challenges the prompt rather than confirming it, and a conceptually precise link.
