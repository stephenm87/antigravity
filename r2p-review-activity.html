Question 1
Which year and document formally established R2P as an international norm?
R2P was unanimously endorsed by 150+ heads of state at the 2005 UN World Summit. It was a political commitment, not a binding treaty — a key reason enforcement remains contested.
Question 2
Pillar III of R2P allows the international community to take action — but only under what condition?
Pillar III requires a "manifest failure" by the state AND UN Security Council authorisation — making great-power vetoes a structural barrier to implementation.
Question 3
The Oxford Academic article argues R2P faces "false death, grave crisis." What does "false death" mean in this context?
"False death" suggests that while states bypass R2P's procedural rules, they still feel compelled to invoke humanitarian language — proof the norm hasn't truly died; it's evolving (a Constructivist reading).
Question 4
What was the official US justification for Operation Absolute Resolve in Venezuela?
The US never formally invoked R2P. The framing was counter-narcotics and state security — blurring whether Venezuela was a humanitarian intervention or unilateral hegemonic action. This ambiguity is core to the analytical debate.
Question 5
Which UN Charter article do critics cite when arguing the Venezuela and Iran operations were illegal?
Article 2(4) prohibits the use or threat of force against another state's territorial integrity or political independence. Critics argue both operations violated this foundational principle of the post-WWII international order.
Question 6
Operation Epic Fury / Roaring Lion targeted Iran. What was the primary stated military objective?
The strikes were preemptive — aimed at Iran's nuclear infrastructure and its leadership chain including Supreme Leader Khamenei. This is why critics argue it cannot be described as R2P: R2P applies to genocide/war crimes, not nuclear proliferation.
Question 7
A Constructivist would most likely focus on which aspect of the Iran strikes?
Constructivists study how norms, identity, and language constitute international politics. The framing of strikes as "liberating" the Iranian people borrows R2P logic — showing how normative language shapes the legitimacy of action even when legal requirements are ignored.
Question 8
Which of the four R2P "trigger" situations was most plausibly argued in the Venezuela case?
Venezuela's 2015–2026 crisis involved extrajudicial killings by the FAES security forces and humanitarian collapse — potentially constituting crimes against humanity. However, the US framed the operation as counter-narcotics, not humanitarian, making R2P applicability ambiguous.
Question 9
A pure Realist explanation of both 2026 interventions would emphasise:
Realists argue states act in their self-interest in an anarchic world. Bypassing the UNSC, asserting hegemony in the Western Hemisphere, and eliminating nuclear rivals are all classically Realist behaviours — regardless of the humanitarian language used.
Question 10
The tension at the heart of this unit is between two definitions of security. Identify the correct pairing:
This is the core HL Security Challenge distinction. In both 2026 cases, the US invoked state security (counter-terror, nuclear threat) while critics pointed to the failure to advance human security — and in Iran, arguably making it worse.

Analytical Review · ~6 min Case Study Comparison Table

Study the table. Use it to spot the patterns across both cases — these are the argumentative building blocks for Paper 2.

🔑

What to notice

Both operations bypassed the UNSC. Both invoked partial humanitarian language. Both were justified by Realist self-interest. The difference lies in what four criteria R2P required — and how far each case diverges from them.

Criterion 🌎 Venezuela (Jan 2026) 💥 Iran (Feb 2026)
R2P trigger? Possible — crimes against humanity (extrajudicial killings, starvation). Not formally declared. No — nuclear proliferation is not one of R2P's four trigger crimes.
UNSC authorisation? ❌ None — entirely unilateral US operation. ❌ None — bilateral US-Israel strike, bypassing UNSC entirely.
Stated justification Counter-narcotics / narco-terrorism (US federal indictments). Not explicitly humanitarian. Preemptive defence + nuclear non-proliferation. "Liberation" framing added rhetorically.
Humanitarian language used? Partial — implicit: "restoring democracy," removing a criminal regime. Yes — explicit "liberation of the Iranian people" rhetoric tracks R2P language.
Human security outcome? Contested — removal of Maduro may improve long-term prospects; short-term chaos possible. Negative — mass civilian casualties, regional retaliatory strikes, destabilisation.
Best theory lens Realism regional hegemony; Constructivism "narco-terror" label strips sovereignty Realism preemptive self-help; Constructivism liberation framing borrows R2P discourse
R2P verdict Ambiguous — humanitarian crisis existed but procedure ignored. Counter-R2P in practice. Not R2P — wrong trigger, no authorisation, human security arguably worsened.
