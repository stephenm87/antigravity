💡 Why AMR²?

Q2b asks you to "propose a solution" — but generic solutions ("the UN should help") score 1-2 marks. The AMR² formula ensures your recommendation is specific, grounded, and self-critical. Examiners reward students who think like policymakers, not commentators.

Understanding the Mark Scheme

2 The 3-Tier Ladder

Your policy recommendation gets graded on a 3-level heuristic. Here's exactly what separates a 2-mark generic answer from a 6-mark scholar response.

Level 1 · Generic ❌ 1-2 Marks

Vague, short, no specific actor or mechanism. Could apply to any issue anywhere. Examiners call this "tourist analysis."

"The international community should help Haiti by sending support and resources to improve the situation."
🔼 Elevation Move: Replace "the international community" with a specific actor; replace "help" with a concrete mechanism.

Level 2 · Surgical ✅ 3-4 Marks

Contains a specific actor, a concrete mechanism, and a clear rationale. This is a solid 4-mark answer, but it doesn't acknowledge complexity.

"The UNSC should mandate the Gang Suppression Force to embed Haitian National Police officers in all operational units, in order to build local capacity and ensure the mission transitions to Haitian-led security within 18 months."
🔼 Elevation Move: Add a risk acknowledgment — what happens if Kenya or other contributors refuse? What's the plan if it doesn't work?

Level 3 · Scholar 🎉 5-6 Marks

Includes a specific actor, a concrete mechanism, a clear rationale, and acknowledges a risk or limitation with a mitigation strategy. This is the 7-band answer.

"The UNSC should mandate the GSF to embed Haitian National Police in all operational units to build local capacity and transition to Haitian-led security within 18 months. However, this risks being undermined if the transitional government lacks the fiscal capacity to sustain enlarged police forces — which could be mitigated by the EABRD pairing the mandate with a 5-year policing infrastructure fund."

🌱 Western Sahara Technology + Equality

The European Commission, through its Directorate-General for Energy (DG ENER), should amend the Delegated Act on Renewable Hydrogen (2023) to require all imported hydrogen to carry a certificate of geographic origin verified by an independent auditor, making hydrogen produced on UN-classified non-self-governing territory ineligible for the EU's green taxonomy. This directly enforces the EU Court of Justice's 2024 ruling that trade agreements cannot legally extend to Western Sahara without Sahrawi consent, and is technically feasible since the EU already operates origin-tracing for agricultural products under Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) regulations. Without such a mechanism, European energy companies will continue signing agreements, like the $12 billion in preliminary contracts already announced, that effectively finance Morocco's territorial claims while denying Sahrawi communities revenue-sharing or employment. Independent monitors have confirmed that local populations received minimal benefit from existing solar installations in the southern provinces, meaning green energy development is replicating the extractive dynamics it claims to replace. However, blocking Western Sahara-origin hydrogen risks removing up to 8 GW of planned capacity from EU supply projections, potentially delaying the Green Deal's 2030 climate targets. To mitigate this, the Commission should fast-track alternative hydrogen partnerships with Namibia's Hyphen project, Chile's HyEx initiative, and Algeria's Sonatrach programme, diversifying supply away from politically contested sourcing while maintaining decarbonization timelines.

🏛 ACTOR"The European Commission, through its Directorate-General for Energy (DG ENER)" → names both the institution and the specific sub-unit responsible
⚙️ MECHANISM"amend the Delegated Act on Renewable Hydrogen (2023) to require all imported hydrogen to carry a certificate of geographic origin verified by an independent auditor" → cites specific legislation and describes an actionable, verifiable policy step
💡 RATIONALE"This directly enforces the EU Court of Justice's 2024 ruling… is technically feasible since the EU already operates origin-tracing… Independent monitors have confirmed that local populations received minimal benefit" → justifies with legal precedent, operational feasibility, and on-the-ground evidence

🛡 MITIGATION"fast-track alternative hydrogen partnerships with Namibia's Hyphen project, Chile's HyEx initiative, and Algeria's Sonatrach programme" → names three specific alternative projects to offset supply risk

⛏ Nicaragua Poverty + Environment

The International Council on Mining and Metals (ICMM) should amend its Mining Principles (2020) to require all member companies holding concessions overlapping indigenous territories to complete an independent Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC) audit under ILO Convention 169 before commencing operations, with non-compliant firms facing suspension from membership and exclusion from ESG-rated investment portfolios. This is urgent because 13 Chinese-owned concessions covering over 6,600 km² of Miskito and Mayangna territory were granted in just 190 days without any documented community consultation, and Mongabay's 2025 investigation confirmed that displacement is already occurring as mining operations encroach on protected biosphere reserves and UNESCO-recognised indigenous homelands. The concessions overlap areas where communities depend on subsistence agriculture and artisanal fishing, meaning extraction does not simply inconvenience residents but eliminates their economic livelihood entirely. However, the Ortega-Murillo regime may block independent auditors from entering the country, as it demonstrated when expelling over 3,000 civil society organisations between 2018 and 2024 and withdrawing Nicaragua from the OAS in 2023. To mitigate this, the ICMM should coordinate with the US SEC and the EU's Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive to embed FPIC verification into supply-chain reporting requirements for companies importing Nicaraguan gold, effectively shifting enforcement from domestic courts to import-jurisdiction regulators who can impose financial penalties on non-compliant refiners in Dubai and Switzerland.

🏛 ACTOR"The International Council on Mining and Metals (ICMM)" → names a specific international industry body with regulatory power over member companies
⚙️ MECHANISM"amend its Mining Principles (2020) to require… an independent FPIC audit under ILO Convention 169… with non-compliant firms facing suspension from membership" → cites the framework, the legal standard, and the enforcement consequence
💡 RATIONALE"13 Chinese-owned concessions covering over 6,600 km² were granted in just 190 days without any documented community consultation… extraction does not simply inconvenience residents but eliminates their economic livelihood entirely" → uses specific data and explains real-world impact

🏴 Haiti Security + Equality

The UN Security Council, under Chapter VII authority, should mandate the Multinational Security Support mission to embed Haitian National Police officers in all operational units at a minimum 1:3 ratio, overseen by a Haitian Community Oversight Board (HCOB) holding quarterly public hearings broadcast on national radio, with an independent ombudsman reporting directly to the Security Council's Haiti sanctions committee. This accountability mechanism is essential because MINUSTAH (2004 to 2017) demonstrates that military effectiveness without local legitimacy collapses; its cholera epidemic killed over 10,000 Haitians, and sexual exploitation scandals destroyed public trust so thoroughly that communities now view external forces with deep suspicion despite 5,600 people being killed in gang violence in 2024 alone. The current MSS mission operates without a formal Chapter VII mandate, relying on bilateral agreements that lack the accountability mechanisms of traditional peacekeeping, which means there is currently no institutional path for Haitian civilians to report misconduct or demand operational changes. However, armed gangs controlling approximately 80% of Port-au-Prince, including the national port and main highway corridors, may exploit consultation timelines to consolidate territorial control during the oversight transition. To mitigate this, the Security Council should authorize a phased approach: immediate security operations for 90 days, followed by the HCOB assuming oversight of all subsequent operations. The Peacebuilding Commission should pair this with a 5-year policing infrastructure fund to build the institutional capacity that external interventions consistently fail to create.

~200 words · ≈18 min handwritten

🏛 ACTOR"The UN Security Council, under Chapter VII authority" → specifies both the institution and the legal basis for its authority to act
⚙️ MECHANISM"mandate the MSS to embed Haitian National Police officers at a minimum 1:3 ratio, overseen by a HCOB holding quarterly public hearings, with an independent ombudsman reporting to the sanctions committee" → describes a concrete, multi-layered accountability structure
💡 RATIONALE"MINUSTAH demonstrates that military effectiveness without local legitimacy collapses… the current MSS operates without a formal Chapter VII mandate… there is currently no institutional path for civilians to report misconduct" → uses historical precedent and identifies the specific governance gap
⚠️ RISK"armed gangs controlling approximately 80% of Port-au-Prince, including the national port and main highway corridors, may exploit consultation timelines" → identifies a specific, quantified operational threat
🛡 MITIGATION"a phased approach: immediate security operations for 90 days, followed by the HCOB assuming oversight… The Peacebuilding Commission should pair this with a 5-year policing infrastructure fund" → separates short-term security from long-term institution-building, naming a second actor

💧 Syr Darya Environment + Security

The Interstate Coordination Water Commission (ICWC), partnering with GIZ and the Eurasian Development Bank, should replace Soviet-era allocation baselines with a dynamic formula calibrated to IPCC glacial melt projections, monitored by 137 automated hydro-stations providing real-time flow data to all three riparian states through a shared digital dashboard. This reform is urgent because Central Asian glaciers have retreated by approximately 30% since 1990, meaning current allocations systematically overpromise water that no longer exists. Downstream Kazakhstan's share increasingly fails to reach the Northern Aral Sea, threatening the $85 million World Bank-funded Kok-Aral Dam restoration that represents the region's most significant environmental recovery effort. Climate projections suggest that by 2050, glacial meltwater contribution to the Syr Darya will decline by an additional 40%, meaning the timeline for achieving a binding scientific mechanism is compressing rapidly. The 2025 Trilateral Protocol signed in Astana provides political will, but without an enforceable allocation formula, goodwill alone cannot prevent future crises. However, Uzbekistan, the basin's largest water consumer at 3.347 billion m³ annually, may resist reduced allocations as a sovereignty threat, particularly given its historical resistance to external monitoring of irrigation practices. To mitigate this, the EADB should fund drip-irrigation technology transfers to Uzbekistan's Fergana Valley, where pilot programs show 40 to 60% water savings with increased crop yields, transforming a zero-sum dispute into a modernization partnership.

⚙️ MECHANISM"replace Soviet-era allocation baselines with a dynamic formula calibrated to IPCC glacial melt projections, monitored by 137 automated hydro-stations providing real-time flow data through a shared digital dashboard" → describes a specific, data-driven system with a transparency component

⚙️ Mechanism Templates

These are the policy "verbs" that work across any topic. On exam day, slot in the actor from your bank and the issue from the stimulus.

Amend an existing framework

[Actor] should amend [existing treaty/regulation/resolution] to require [specific new obligation] verified by [enforcement body or audit mechanism].

Create certification or conditionality

[Actor] should require all [companies/states/organizations] to obtain a [certificate/audit/compliance report] before accessing [market/funding/membership].

Link market access to compliance

[Actor] should condition [trade access/development funding/investment eligibility] on demonstrated compliance with [standard/convention/protocol], with non-compliant parties facing [specific consequence].

Establish an oversight body

[Actor] should create a [board/commission/monitoring unit] comprising [specific stakeholders], with the authority to [review/approve/veto specific actions].

⚠️ Risk-Mitigation Pairs

The "However..." pivot is the hardest part. Most exam risks fall into one of four categories. Prepare a mitigation pattern for each.

⚠️ Sovereignty resistance
"State Y may frame this as external interference, undermining domestic support for cooperation."
🛡 Mitigation: embed local actors
Pair the policy with a local oversight body, joint committee, or phased transition to national leadership so the reform is owned, not imposed.
⚠️ Economic disruption
"This risks disrupting supply chains, raising commodity prices, or threatening jobs in the affected sector."
🛡 Mitigation: diversify or compensate
Name alternative suppliers, transition funds, or technology transfers that make the reform economically viable rather than punitive.
⚠️ Enforcement gap
"The target state may block auditors, expel NGOs, or refuse to comply, rendering the policy unverifiable on the ground."
🛡 Mitigation: shift jurisdiction
Embed compliance into import-side regulations (e.g., SEC reporting, EU due diligence directives) so enforcement happens in home markets, not the target state.
⚠️ Timeline conflict
"Consultation processes may delay urgent action, allowing the crisis to escalate during the implementation window."
🛡 Mitigation: phased approach
Separate immediate stabilization (first 90 days under existing authority) from longer-term governance reform, so neither track blocks the other.

🎯 Exam Day Strategy

You have approximately 15 minutes for Q2b. Here is how to allocate that time.

3 min
Read and decide. Scan the stimulus. Identify which GPC it connects to most strongly. Select the actor from your bank that fits best. Do not overthink this; your first instinct is usually correct.
3 min
Write Actor + Mechanism. Draft the opening sentence using one of your mechanism templates. This is the core of your answer and what earns the most marks. Name the actor with sub-unit and describe one concrete, verifiable policy step.
3 min
Write the Rationale. Connect the stimulus evidence to your own knowledge. Why is this policy feasible? Cite a precedent, an existing system that proves it can work, or data from the stimulus that justifies urgency.
3 min
Write Risk + Mitigation. Start with "However" and name one credible risk from your prepared pairs. Then propose a specific mitigation that names a secondary actor or alternative mechanism.
3 min
Review and tighten. Reread your paragraph. Check that you named a specific actor (not "the UN"), cited a real mechanism, gave evidence, and addressed risk. Cut any filler sentences that do not add marks.

🔥 Recommended Drill

Take three different stimulus extracts and write only the opening sentence (Actor + Mechanism) for each, using a different GPC each time. Time yourself to 2 minutes per sentence. This trains the pivot skill. Once the opening sentence is automatic, the rest of the paragraph follows naturally.
