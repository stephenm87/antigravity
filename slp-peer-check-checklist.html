Part 1: Design
Visual Structure & Layout
Criteria✗OK☺Self
Hierarchy
The most important information is the largest and easiest to notice.
Alignment
Elements are well aligned and the layout looks organised and intentional.
Set Consistency
All 3 infographics look like a set (similar style, format, layout, or visual language).
Typography
Criteria✗OK☺Self
Font choice
Text is clear, easy to read, and appropriate for an infographic.
Typography control
Font sizes, spacing, and styles are tidy and consistent across all 3 infographics.
Colour
Criteria✗OK☺Self
Colour choice
Colours work well together and are suitable for the topic.
Colour for meaning
Colour helps understanding and does not distract from the information.
Visual Communication
Criteria✗OK☺Self
Show more than explain
The infographics use visuals (icons, charts, images) effectively rather than relying only on text.
Data visualisation
Charts, graphs, or statistics are presented clearly and accurately (especially in the Impact infographic).
Part 2: Content
Project Clarity
Criteria✗OK☺Self
Is it clear what the service learning project is?
Is it clear what the student's intentions and goals are?
Is it clear how many times the student participated?
Is it clear who the audience is and how many people were impacted?
Authenticity & Accuracy
Criteria✗OK☺Self
The information feels authentic, honest, and realistic.
Data and statistics look believable - nothing seems exaggerated or fabricated.
Specific Infographic Checks
Infographic 1: My Impact
Criteria✗OK☺Self
The data is realistic and easy to understand.
The methodology (how data was collected) is clear or implied.
Infographic 2: My Process
Criteria✗OK☺Self
The process is explained in enough detail (not just surface-level).
The reader can follow the steps from start to finish.
Infographic 3: My Growth
Criteria✗OK☺Self
The reflection shows meaningful depth and is not too generic.
The student explains what they learned or how they changed, not just what they did.
Evidence & Documentation
Criteria✗OK☺Self
There are 2 or more quotes included.
There are 5 or more images from the event.
The student's full name appears on each infographic.
Each infographic has a clear title that tells the reader what it is about.
