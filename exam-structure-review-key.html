Know Your Exam: Papers 1, 2 & 3

Correct mark values, objectives, and optimal response formats for all questions across Paper 1, Paper 2, and Paper 3 HL.

📄 Paper 1 — 25 marks 📄 Paper 2 — 30 marks (2 × 15) 📄 Paper 3 HL — 28 marks
P1

Paper 1 — Stimulus-Based Questions

SL & HL · 1 hr 15 min · 4 compulsory questions · 4 provided sources

25
Total Marks
Question Marks Objective & Best Response Format
Context: 4 sources (text, image, data, infographic) from the common core unit Understanding Power and Global Politics. All questions compulsory.
Q1
Question 1
Knowledge & understanding of a source
AO1
3
 Objective
Identify and describe a specific feature, claim, or message from one named source. This is a comprehension question — close reading only, no argumentation required.
Best Response Format
2–3 sentences only. Direct identification → quote or paraphrase from the source → one brief explanatory sentence. Do NOT add own knowledge. Do NOT write an introduction.
Q2
Question 2
Application & analysis of a source
AO2
4
 Objective
Apply knowledge to explain how a concept, claim, or term from one source connects to a broader political context. You must go beyond simply describing the source — analysis and your own knowledge are expected.
Best Response Format
PEE structure — one well-developed paragraph: Point (clear claim) → Evidence (quote or data from the source) → Explain (why it matters politically, with reference to own knowledge). Do not write multiple disconnected points.
Q3
Question 3
Compare and/or contrast two sources
AO3
6
 Objective
Compare AND/OR contrast the views, arguments, or information presented in two specific named sources. The focus is the relationship between sources — not their individual content in isolation.
Best Response Format
2–3 distinct comparison points. For each: Similarity or difference → Evidence/quote from Source X → Evidence/quote from Source Y → Significance of the comparison. Use signal phrases: "While Source A argues…, Source B contends…" Do NOT summarise each source separately.
Q4
Question 4
Evaluate & synthesize all sources + own knowledge
AO3AO4
12
 Objective
Evaluate the extent of a stated claim by synthesizing all four sources with your own knowledge into a sustained, balanced argument. This is the highest-order question — you must argue, not describe. All four sources must appear.
Best Response Format
Mini-essay: Thesis (clear position on "to what extent") → 2–3 body paragraphs (argument + source evidence + own knowledge + analysis) → Counterargument paragraph → Conclusion with a nuanced verdict. Do not sacrifice Q4 time to perfect Q1–Q3.
25

Total: 3 + 4 + 6 + 12 = 25 marks
Q4 is worth 12 of the 25 marks — nearly half the paper. Students should budget approximately 35 minutes for Q4 alone. If running short on time, move to Q4 immediately — a well-argued incomplete Q4 outscores a polished Q3 every time.
Paper 1  25 marks  ·  1 hr 15 min  ·  SL & HL
P2

Paper 2 — Extended Response Essays

SL & HL · 1 hr 45 min · 1 essay from Section A + 1 essay from Section B

30
Total (2 × 15)
Section / Essay Marks each Objective & Best Response Format
Structure: Two sections. Students choose one question from Section A and one from Section B — 2 essays total. Each essay is independently marked at 15 marks.
A
Section A — Thematic Essay
Rights & Justice / Development & Sustainability / Peace & Conflict
AO2AO3
15
 Objective
Write a focused analytical essay on one thematic study. Demonstrate knowledge, critical analysis, and evaluation through relevant case studies and conceptual arguments. A clear thesis and balanced evaluation are expected.
Best Response Format
10 min plan → thesis + 3 arguments + evidence per argument. Essay: Introduction (define key concept + thesis with clear position) → 2–3 body paragraphs (PEEL: claim → case study evidence → analysis → link to thesis) → Conclusion (restate position, acknowledge nuance, broader significance).
B
Section B — Integrating Essay
Connections across thematic studies and the core unit required
AO2AO3AO4
15
 Objective
Write an essay that explicitly integrates across thematic studies and the core unit. The argument itself must be integrating — it cannot stay within a single theme. Cross-theme connections are not optional, they are the assessment criterion.
Best Response Format
Same structure as Section A, BUT: thesis and each body paragraph must draw connections across themes (e.g., how a Rights claim intersects with Development dynamics or Peace & Conflict). Use one integrating concept as the spine of the argument. Mentioning multiple themes without connecting them does not earn integration marks.
Sec A vs Sec B — key difference: —
Section A goes deep on ONE theme; Section B requires you to weave multiple themes together. In Section B, the integration IS the argument — not just mentioning different themes in separate paragraphs, but showing how they interact and inform each other.
Common command terms: —
Evaluate — balanced judgment, evidence for AND against, clear conclusion on extent. To what extent — argue about degree/scope and defend it with evidence. Discuss — explore multiple perspectives, present and examine different views. Examine — analyze in detail from multiple angles; go beyond description.
Paper 2  30 marks (2 × 15)  ·  1 hr 45 min  ·  SL & HL
P3

Paper 3 — HL Extension (Stimulus + Questions)

HL Only · 1 hr 30 min · One stimulus source · 4 compulsory questions: Q1, Q2a, Q2b, Q3

28
Total Marks
Question Marks Objective & Best Response Format
Context: One stimulus source related to the 8 GPCs. Students apply their own researched case studies — the examiner knows nothing about their specific research.
Q1
Question 1
Stimulus comprehension — identify/describe from source
AO1
3
 Objective
Identify or describe a specific feature, claim, or argument directly from the stimulus source. Comprehension only — no own knowledge, no argument-making.
Best Response Format
2–3 sentences max. Direct identification → quote or paraphrase precisely from the source → one brief explanatory sentence. Stay entirely within the source. Do not introduce your case study here.
2a
Question 2a — Case Study Bridge
Analyze a political issue from researched case study connected to the stimulus
AO2
4
 Objective
Analyze a specific political issue from a case study the student has independently researched that connects to the GPC and theme raised by the stimulus. Bridge prior research to the exam context.
Best Response Format
GPC identification (name it) → Case study context (who/what/where/when — 1 sentence) → Specific evidence or data point → Analytical explanation (why this is a political issue and how it connects to the GPC) → 1 linking sentence connecting back to the stimulus theme. Choose your case study now and keep it for Q2b.
2b
Question 2b — Policy Recommendation
Propose a policy addressing the Q2a issue — actor, mechanism, rationale, risks
AO2AO3
6
 Objective
Propose and justify a specific, feasible policy recommendation to address the political issue identified in Q2a. Must include who acts, how, why it works, and what challenges or unintended consequences exist. Must build on the same case study as Q2a.
Best Response Format
AMR² structure: Actor (who implements it?) → Mechanism (specific policy action) → Rationale (why it works — link to source + case study evidence) → Risks/unintended consequences (at least one, specifically articulated) → brief mitigation or concluding sentence. Do NOT switch case study from Q2a.
Q3
Question 3 — HL Extension Essay
"To what extent…?" essay · 2+ GPCs · sustained argument required
AO2AO3AO4
15
 Objective
Write a sustained "To what extent…?" evaluative essay drawing on independently researched case studies and explicitly referencing two or more named GPCs. Sustained argument, counterargument, and nuanced conclusion are all required. This is the highest-order HL question.
Best Response Format
5 min plan: define key concept + thesis (your extent claim) + arguments + GPC links + counterargument.
Essay: Introduction (define concept, state thesis with explicit extent) → 2–3 body paragraphs (GPC name + case study evidence + analysis + link to thesis) → Counterargument (different GPC or perspective) → Conclusion (nuanced "to what extent" verdict — never just "yes" or "no"). Every paragraph must argue, never merely describe.
28

Total: 3 + 4 + 6 + 15 = 28 marks
Q3 (15 marks) is worth more than Q1 + Q2a + Q2b combined (13 marks total). Students must budget approximately 45 minutes for Q3 and move to it on time even if Q2b isn't perfect. A complete Q3 with a genuine counterargument will always outscore a polished but unfinished one.

Q2a → Q2b link: Q2b must build on Q2a using the same case study. Students should lock in their case study choice before beginning Q2a. Switching contexts between Q2a and Q2b costs marks and time.
Paper 3  28 marks  ·  1 hr 30 min  ·  HL only
ΣSummary Answers — Marks & Weighting
Paper 1
25 marks
Which question is worth most, and how long?
Q4 (12 marks) — budget ~35 min of 75. It is worth nearly half the paper. Q1–Q3 combined = only 13 marks. Prioritize Q4 ruthlessly.
Paper 2
30 marks
Key difference between Section A and Section B?
Section A = deep focus on ONE theme. Section B = argument must integrate ACROSS multiple themes. Integration is not a bonus in Section B — it IS the criterion.
Paper 3 — HL Only
28 marks
Q3 vs Q1 — how do they differ?
Q1 (3 marks): comprehension only — describe what you see in the source. Q3 (15 marks): sustained evaluative essay using own case studies + 2+ GPCs. Q3 requires synthesis, argument, counterargument, and a nuanced verdict.
